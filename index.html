An Unusual Career Path

I began my career as an artist. I was born and raised in Upstate New York and was in college studying painting at Syracuse University when I traveled west and then north for a summer commercial fishing in Alaska. This experience left me with a taste for adventure. After completing college and after a short stint as a furniture maker in Syracuse, I traveled west again and spent five years fighting forest fires for the U.S. Federal Government. During those five years I wintered with my wife in New Orleans and later in Upstate New York until I followed her permanently to Cambridge, Massachusetts. There I spent several years working in higher ed. management and as a teaching assistant in statistics at Harvard University. I eventually enrolled in graduate school at UMass Amherst, where I studied business and public policy to earn an MBA and MPPA dual-masters degree. This led to my work in operations and systems at Jumpstart, a national non-profit based in Boston, my work in finance at Ford Motor Company in Dearborn, Michigan, and my subsequent several years at EMC Corporation, a global technology firm now owned by Dell Technologies, supporting strategic operational programs and eventually managing the companyâ€™s consulting business with two global financial services customers in New York City.
