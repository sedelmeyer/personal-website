My Unusual Career

I began my career as an artist. I was born and raised in Upstate New York and was in college studying painting at Syracuse University when I traveled west and then north for a summer commercial fishing in Alaska. This experience left me with a taste for adventure. After completing college and after a short stint as a furniture maker in Syracuse, I traveled west again and spent five years fighting forest fires for the U.S. Federal Government. During this time I wintered back east with my wife (New Orlean and later Upstate New York) until I followed her permanently (and all year round) to Cambridge, Massachusetts. There I spent several years working in higher ed. management (and as a teaching assistant in statistics) at Harvard University. I eventually enrolled in graduate school at UMass Amherst, where I studied business, public policy and innovation to earn both an MBA and MPPA degree. This led to my work in operations and systems at Jumpstart, a national non-profit based in Boston, my work in finance at Ford Motor Company in Dearborn, Michigan, and my subsequent several years at EMC Corporation, a global technology firm now owned by Dell Technologies, supporting strategic operational programs and eventually managing the companyâ€™s consulting business for two global financial services customers in New York City.
